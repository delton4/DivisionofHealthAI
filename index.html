Mission Protocol

Engineering AI that anticipates deterioration and personalizes care.

We build machine learning and deep learning systems for early diagnosis, disease severity assessment, and adaptive therapy across healthcare. Our research spans bioelectronic medicine, neural interfaces, and clinical AI at scale.

Early diagnosis intelligence

Predictive models that surface subtle signals before clinical deterioration escalates.

Severity & personalization

Adaptive algorithms that stratify risk and tailor interventions to each patient.

Bioelectronic medicine

Data-driven neuromodulation that links nervous system signals to immune and inflammatory control.

Research Focus

Multimodal intelligence for complex clinical environments.

Our programs span point-of-care decision support, wearable monitoring, clinical phenotyping, and maternal-neonatal risk prediction.

In-hospital Deterioration AI

Point-of-care

Trained on >1.5M hospitalizations using 18 EHR features to predict deaths, ICU transfers, and RRT calls with >90% accuracy, outperforming the Epic Deterioration Model.

Wearable Deterioration Prediction

Wearables

RNN model trained on >1.3k clinical patches achieves >94% sensitivity and ~17-hour lead time for clinical alerts; algorithm is wearable-agnostic.

Clinical Phenotyping

Unsupervised

Unsupervised learning clusters patients into phenotypes (lung inflammation, kidney dysfunction, immune) to explain outcomes and optimize clinical trial recruitment.

Maternal Fever Prediction

Monitoring

Continuous vitals from 336 laboring patients, with HRV as a key signal, predict fever 2-3 hours before onset, supporting neonatal sepsis prevention.
